(i) before agreeing on the unit price charged by BGI for providing the Services,
(a) the Group had reviewed the unit price charged by BGI for providing
similar services to independent third parties to ensure that the agreed unit
price was not higher than the unit price charged by BGI when providing
similar services to independent third parties; and (b) the Group had also
reviewed the unit price(s) charged by other independent service providers
which were higher than the unit price charged by BGI;

favourable to SDCL than the fee for similar services offered to independent

(iv) the independent non-executive Directors will conduct an annual review of the

interests of the Company and its Shareholders as a whole; and
(v) the Company’s external auditors will conduct an annual review on the
continuing connected transactions under the Master Services Agreement.

REASONS FOR AND BENEFITS OF THE ENTERING INTO OF THE MASTER
SERVICES AGREEMENT AND THE SALE AND PURCHASE AGREEMENT
The Group is principally engaged in (i) provision of tumor immune cell therapy, immune
cell storage and health management services in the PRC; (ii) manufacture, research and
development, sale and distribution of health related and pharmaceutical products in the
PRC and Hong Kong; (iii) provision of medical laboratory testing services and health
check services in Hong Kong; and (iv) trading of securities in Hong Kong.
The Company has partnered with BGI to incorporate SDCL in June 2020 for the
provision of medical laboratory testing services in Hong Kong.
Prior to the commencement of the Universal Community Testing Programme, BGI had
provided the COVID-19 test service to SDCL for COVID-19 testing for private clinics
and corporate and government clients. Such cooperation between BGI and SDCL was on
a pilot run basis and the scale of the fee for the provision of the Services by BGI to
SDCL had not been formally discussed and agreed.
As disclosed in the announcement dated 16 September 2020 of the Company, SDCL has
provided COVID-19 nucleic acid testing service to Hong Kong citizens under the
Universal Community Testing Programme and SDCL has completed more than 1.6
million tests under the Universal Community Testing Programme. As the timeline for
the Universal Community Testing Programme was extremely tight and SDCL had to
equip its laboratories and prepare for over a million expected COVID-19 tests in an
extremely short period of time, BGI, as a 40% shareholder of SDCL, had the capacity
and expertise and was willing to assist SDCL in the Universal Community Testing
Programme by supplying the Equipment and providing the Services before the terms of
supplying the Equipment and providing the Services could be negotiated and agreed in
order to meet the tight timeline. Apart from BGI, SDCL was unable to identify any other
service provider or equipment supplier which was able to provide or supply similar
services or similar equipment in such a large scale within a very short period of time.
8
After completion of the Universal Community Testing Programme, BGI and SDCL have
agreed on the terms of supplying the Equipment and providing the Services where the
scale of the Services provided in the Universal Community Testing Programme had also
been taken into account in determining the unit price. BGI and SDCL therefore entered
into the Master Services Agreement and the Sale and Purchase Agreement. The Board
considered that the Services and the Equipment have enabled SDCL to conduct the
COVID-19 testing under the Universal Community Testing Programme successfully in a
very tight timeframe and allowed the Group to further expand its business in the medical
laboratory testing and health check services.
Having considered the factors as mentioned above, the Directors (including the
independent non-executive Directors) are of the view that the Master Services
Agreement and the Sale and Purchase Agreement were entered into on normal
commercial terms and in the ordinary and usual course of business of the Group, and the
terms thereof, including the Service Fee under the Master Services Agreement and the
purchase price under the Sale and Purchase Agreement are fair and reasonable and in the
interests of the Company and its Shareholders as a whole.
None of the Directors of the Company has material interest in the Master Services
Agreement and the Sale and Purchase Agreement and the transactions contemplated
thereunder respectively and hence no Director is required to abstain from voting on the
relevant resolutions of the Board approving the same.
